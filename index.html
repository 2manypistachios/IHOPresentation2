US Healthcare Intervention

By IHO

1. US annual health care cost is greater than $3,6 trillion, about 17% of the US GDP

2. Waste in the US health care has been estimated to be at least about ¼ of the above amount.



3. The number of uninsured Americans is about $28 million and is growing at the rate of about 0.5%/year







4. There is only limited number of options to cover the uninsured:

  • mandatory insurance requirements
  • high deductible plans
  • high costs of insurance policies
  • higher taxes
  • increased national debt.
  • Each of these options has many opponents.

    • 5. In 2018, the overall Medicare cost was $582 billion. In 2018, there were about 55 million Medicare members. Thus, the annual cost of Medicare per member in 2018 was about $10,600.

    6. To cover all 28 million uninsured Americans one would need an additional 28,000,000 × $10600 ≈ $296 billion annually.
